Harvard Kennedy School

Data-driven Decision-making Methods and Procedures

The big data approach to urban decision-making has become increasingly prevalent. However, we have yet limited understanding of how organizations can effectively translate its potential into both social and economic value. Considering the growing reliance on data we should be more critical about the processes of data creation and interpretation. Data is a strategic asset, but it′s only valuable if it′s used constructively and appropriately to deliver results. We seek to understand the potential shortcomings in these processes by interrogating the current flow of data-driven decision-making.
